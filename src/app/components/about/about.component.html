About me

Originally self-taught on the job

My introduction to programming came in the real world on the job. While working as a Team Lead for a team of Reporting Analyst at Disney, a member of my team showed me how to access VBA code that was generated by recording macros in MS Excel. I began by first recording small macros to achieve small efficiencies, then I took the code generated from the smaller macros and combined the code together to create very quick, very accurate, and very efficient automation of report generation in Excel.

Getting my Associate’s Degree in 2020

Over the years I’ve learned many new technologies and programming languages, enough so that I can quickly get up to speed on any new technology or language. Still, I feel that to be the best developer I can be, and to be competitive, it’s really best to have a well-rounded education that is complete with a degree. So, after I finish my AA in Programming, I will start working on my Bachelor’s Degree in Application Development.

I love challenges, solutions more so

As with anything, developing websites and web applications takes time and patience as there are always new challenges to overcome. But that’s one of the main reasons I love being a developer. So, I constantly struggle with new challenges in order to grow.
